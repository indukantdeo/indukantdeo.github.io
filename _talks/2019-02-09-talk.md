Over the past few years, the advancement in computing power led to a multitude of high-fidelity fluid datasets. With the accessibility to high fidelity datasets, the scientists begin to ponder about the new methodologies to model the turbulence, departing from the traditional approach of turbulence modelling guided by physical intuition and empiricism. Unfortunately, existing turbulence models are notoriously inaccurate for flows in complex geometries. Therefore, more accurate predictive models for turbulence are needed. Machine learning algorithms can be employed to extract cause-and-effect relationships directly from large numerical datasets. These correlations can be used to model the turbulence with greater generality and accuracy without the human bias. This talk reviews the state of the art data-driven procedures for the turbulence closure modeling.
